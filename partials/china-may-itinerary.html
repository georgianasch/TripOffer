Day 2 — Thu, 7 May

Arrival & Relaxed Exploration (Jet-lag Friendly) — Stay: Liaoliaoxin Loft Hotel (Gulou)

  • Key planning point: This day is intentionally relaxed to recover from jet lag and adjust to local time.
  • Goal: recover from flight, adjust to local time, and get a gentle introduction to Beijing.
  • 07:30–11:00 (Arrival & morning): check-in, unpack, shower, hydrate, light snack.
  • Optional/rest: skip walking, stay in hotel room, and take a power nap if needed.
  • 11:00–14:00 (Late morning/early afternoon): short walk around Gulou & Drum Tower (5–10 min from hotel), observe local life, cafés, and small shops.
  • Optional/rest: stay in a tea house/hotel café, light reading, and plan next day; distance target ~1–2 km max.
  • 14:00–17:00 (Afternoon): Yandai Xiejie + Houhai Lakes, optional rickshaw ride for minimal effort.
  • Optional/rest: skip full lakes loop, sit at a waterside café, or take short tuk-tuk/taxi hops; distance ~2–3 km if fully active.
  • Tip: keep pace slow and enjoy the surroundings, no need to see everything on Day 1.
  • 17:30–19:30 (Dinner): casual local dinner (dumplings, noodles, or Peking duck).
  • Optional/rest: eat in hotel/café and skip night walk if tired.
  • 20:00 onwards (Wind down): optional short evening walk (Houhai or lit Drum Tower) or return to hotel, shower, and sleep early.

Day 3 — Fri, 8 May

Imperial Beijing + Architecture Museum + Kung Fu Show — Stay: Liaoliaoxin Loft Hotel (Gulou)

  • Theme: Imperial palaces → parks → traditional architecture → evening show.
  • Key planning point: Forbidden City is usually more crowded than Summer Palace, so it is scheduled on Friday.
  • 08:00 — Depart Hotel: walk ~750m (~10 min) to Gulou Dajie Station (Line 8). Tip: light breakfast on the way (soy milk, snacks, jianbing).
  • 08:15 — Metro to Tiananmen: Line 8, transfer at Dongdan to Line 1 Eastbound, exit Tiananmen East/West; ~30–35 min, ~¥6–7, route distance ~10 km.
  • 08:50–11:20 — Forbidden City: book 08:30–09:00 slot for lower crowds; suggested visit ~2.5–3h.
  • Booking: Trip.com Forbidden City (or official Palace Museum site).
  • 11:30–12:00 — Jingshan Park: ~5 min walk north from Forbidden City exit; skyline viewpoint; ~¥10 entry.
  • 12:15–13:15 — Lunch (Qianmen area): Line 2 from Jishuitan to Qianmen, ~15–20 min + short walk; good options for Peking duck, noodles, cafés.
  • 13:30 — Metro to Temple of Heaven: Qianmen (Line 2) → transfer to Line 5 (Tiantandongmen), ~28 min, ~¥3–5; then ~6–7 min walk to entrance.
  • 13:58–15:30 — Temple of Heaven Park: Hall of Prayer, gardens, and local tai chi/music atmosphere; ~1.5h.
  • 15:30 — Move to Beijing Ancient Architecture Museum: taxi/Didi ~10–15 min (~¥15–25, easiest) or metro option (~20–25 min + ~650m walk).
  • 15:50–17:10 — Beijing Ancient Architecture Museum: traditional architecture models (siheyuan, temples, pagodas), light indoor visit; ~1–1.25h; tickets usually available on site.
  • 17:10 — Head to Red Theatre: taxi/Didi ~15–20 min, ~¥30–40 (recommended after full walking day).
  • 19:30–20:40 — Red Theatre Kung Fu Show: Shaolin performance, duration ~70–80 min. Book: red-theater.com
  • Return to hotel: taxi/Didi ~20–30 min, ~¥30–40; expected return around 21:30–22:00.

Day 7 — Tue, 12 May

Shanghai Day 1 — Arrival + Jing’an Local Life (Gentle Start)

  • Travel note: train trip from Beijing to Shanghai on this day.
  • Theme: Soft landing + neighborhood immersion
  • All walkable from hotel
  • Late Afternoon (3–5 PM) — Jing’an Temple: crowd moderate (mostly locals), visit time 45–60 min
  • Why: beautiful golden temple surrounded by modern skyscrapers — classic Shanghai contrast
  • Jing’an Park stroll: right next to the temple, crowd light, 30–40 min
  • What to expect: locals exercising, walking dogs, and a quiet atmosphere
  • Evening dinner — Wujiang Road: casual local food street
  • Try: shengjianbao (pan-fried buns), noodles, skewers
  • Crowd: mostly locals after 7 PM
  • Tip: early night to adjust to time zone

Day 8 — Wed, 13 May

Shanghai Day 2 — French Concession + 1000 Trees + Riverside + Pudong Skyline (Low Crowd Flow)

  • Planning note: 18 May can be swapped with 13 May if energy is good, since Yu Garden is usually less crowded on Wednesday.
  • Theme: Architecture, cafés, art, river — in logical flow.
  • Morning (start 8:30 AM) — French Concession: Wukang Road → Sinan Mansions → tree-lined residential lanes → Sinan Mansions
  • Crowd: light in the morning | Time: 2.5–3h
  • Stop for brunch or coffee — this is Shanghai at its most atmospheric.
  • Midday — M50 Art District: contemporary galleries, photography, experimental art, calm industrial vibe
  • Crowd: light to moderate | Time: 1.5–2h | best before 1 PM for quieter experience
  • Then — 1000 Trees Building: architecture stop near Suzhou Creek for design views, rooftop terraces, and photos (~45–60 min)
  • Late afternoon — North Bund Green Land: lighter crowds than the main Bund, skyline views, 1–1.5h
  • Cross the river to Pudong (Lujiazui): take the Huangpu ferry or metro, then walk among Shanghai Tower, Shanghai World Financial Center, and Jin Mao Tower.
  • Best moment: arrive around sunset for lights-on skyline views and night photos between the high-rise buildings.
  • Only continue to classic The Bund if already nearby and it feels natural.
